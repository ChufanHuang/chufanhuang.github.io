Chufan Huang

Computational social science — networks, misinformation, human–AI interaction, visual media.

I’m a master’s student in Computational Communication at the Institute of Communication and Media Research (IKMZ), University of Zurich, with graduate coursework at ETH Zurich. I study how information flows through platforms and how timed interventions—labels, visual cues, or recommender tweaks—reshape collective attention and individual judgment. A second line of work examines AI-generated media as agenda and credibility signals.

Methodologically, I work end-to-end with digital traces. I build time-varying network models (including ERGMs/SAOMs and structural-virality measures), apply NLP/LLMs and multimodal embeddings (e.g., CLIP; UMAP for projections), and use time-series segmentation/change-point detection to locate intervention windows. Everything lives in reproducible Python/R workflows. Where possible, I layer virtual/online experiments and small field tests on top of observational pipelines to identify mechanisms and estimate impact.

Before Zurich, I earned an M.A. in Journalism & Communication at Sichuan International Studies University, which gave me the theoretical backbone and qualitative depth (mediatization, discourse/CDA, ethnography/interviews, grounded theory).

In short: my first master’s emphasized theory and qualitative interpretation; my current training scales those questions into measurable, testable models with language, network, and time-series methods— moving along a traces → models → experiments arc. (See more under “Research experiences.”)
