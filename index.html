Chufan Huang

Computational social science — networks, misinformation, human–AI interaction, visual media.

I’m Chufan Huang, a master’s student in computational social science & communication at the Institute of Communication and Media Research (IKMZ), University of Zurich, with graduate coursework at ETH Zurich. My work asks a meso-level question: how does information flow through platform networks—and how do those flows shape collective attention and individual judgment? I study news, misinformation, conspiracy narratives, and AI-generated content using network analysis, NLP, and time-series methods.

Before Zurich, I completed an M.A. in Journalism & Communication at Sichuan International Studies University, which gave me the theoretical backbone and qualitative depth (mediatization, discourse/CDA, ethnography/interviews, grounded theory).

In short: my first Master’s emphasized theory & qualitative interpretation; my second Master’s scales those questions into measurable, testable models with NLP, LLMs, SNA, sentiment/stance, and time-series. (See more under “Research experiences.”)
